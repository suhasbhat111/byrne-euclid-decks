\textbf{Book~I.\enspace Prop.\ XVII. Theor.}
\medskip\noindent\itshape
Any two angles of a triangle \drawLine[bottom]{AB,BC,AC} are together less
than two right angles.
\bigskip\noindent\upshape\begin{center}
Produce \drawUnitLine{AC}, then will\\
$\drawAngle{ACB}+\drawAngle{BCD}=\drawTwoRightAngles$.\\[6pt]
But $\drawAngle{BCD}>\drawAngle{A}$~(pr.~I.16)\\[4pt]
$\therefore \drawAngle{ACB}+\drawAngle{A}<\drawTwoRightAngles$,
\end{center}
\noindent and in the same manner it may be shown that any other two angles of
the triangle taken together are less than two right angles.
\begin{flushright}Q.\ E.\ D.\end{flushright}
